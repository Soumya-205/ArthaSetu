# Savings Account

## Overview
A Savings Account is the most basic banking product, allowing customers to deposit and withdraw money while earning a small interest on the balance. It is typically the first product a new customer opens, and most other products (FD, RD, loans) require an active savings account linked to them.

## Eligibility & documents required
- Any individual aged 18 or above can open a savings account independently. Minors can open one jointly with a parent or guardian.
- Documents required: PAN card, Aadhaar card, one passport-size photo, and a filled account-opening form.
- Minimum initial deposit: ₹500 for a regular savings account, ₹0 for a Basic Savings Bank Deposit Account (BSBDA), aimed at first-time or low-income customers.

## Interest rates / returns
- Balance up to ₹1 lakh: 2.7% per annum
- Balance above ₹1 lakh: 3.0% per annum
- Interest is calculated daily on the closing balance and credited quarterly.

## Risks / things to know
- A minimum balance must be maintained (₹1,000 for regular accounts); falling below this may attract a small penalty, except for BSBDA accounts which have no minimum balance requirement.
- Interest earned is taxable if it exceeds ₹10,000 in a financial year, as per income tax rules.
- Savings account interest rates are much lower than FD or RD, so large idle balances are not the most efficient way to save long-term.

## How to apply
- Online: New-to-bank customers can start the process via video KYC on the mobile app, completing identity verification remotely.
- Offline: Visit the nearest branch with required documents; account is typically activated within 1-2 working days.
- Existing customers can request additional accounts (e.g. joint or nominee-added) directly through net banking.
