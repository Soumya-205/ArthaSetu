# Life Insurance

## Overview
A Life Insurance plan provides a lump sum payout to the customer's nominee in case of the policyholder's death during the policy term, offering financial protection for the family. Some plans also include a maturity benefit if the policyholder survives the full term.

## Eligibility & documents required
- Any individual aged 18 to 60 years can apply; some plans extend coverage up to age 65.
- Documents required: PAN card, Aadhaar card, one passport-size photo, income proof (salary slip or ITR), and a basic medical declaration form.
- A medical checkup may be required for higher coverage amounts or older applicants.

## Interest rates / returns
- Coverage amount (sum assured) ranges from ₹5 lakh to ₹1 crore, depending on the plan chosen.
- Premiums are paid monthly, quarterly, or annually, and depend on the customer's age, coverage amount, and policy term (typically 10 to 30 years).
- Term plans (pure protection, no maturity benefit) have lower premiums than endowment plans (protection plus savings).

## Risks / things to know
- If premiums are not paid, the policy may lapse after a grace period, and coverage stops.
- Term insurance has no payout if the policyholder survives the full term — it is pure protection, not an investment.
- Pre-existing medical conditions must be disclosed; non-disclosure can lead to claim rejection.
- Premiums paid are eligible for tax deduction under Section 80C, and the payout is generally tax-free for the nominee.

## How to apply
- Online: Log in to net banking or the mobile app, go to "Insurance," select a plan, fill in the medical declaration, and complete payment.
- Offline: Visit the nearest branch with required documents; branch staff can help compare term vs. endowment plans based on the customer's needs.
- Nearest branch details can be provided by the assistant if requested.
