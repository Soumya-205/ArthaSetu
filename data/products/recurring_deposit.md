# Recurring Deposit

## Overview
A Recurring Deposit (RD) is a savings scheme where a customer deposits a fixed amount every month for a chosen tenure, earning interest similar to a Fixed Deposit. Unlike FD, where the full amount is deposited upfront, RD lets customers build savings gradually through smaller monthly contributions.

## Eligibility & documents required
- Any resident individual with a savings account can open an RD. Minors can open one with a guardian.
- Documents required: PAN card, Aadhaar card, one passport-size photo, and a filled RD application form.
- Minimum monthly deposit: ₹100, with no upper limit. Minimum tenure: 12 months, maximum: 10 years.

## Interest rates / returns
- Tenure 1 to 3 years: 6.5% per annum
- Tenure 3 to 5 years: 6.8% per annum
- Tenure above 5 years: 7.0% per annum
- Senior citizens receive an additional 0.5% on all tenures.
- The final maturity amount depends on the monthly deposit, tenure, and interest rate chosen.

## Risks / things to know
- Missing a monthly installment attracts a small penalty, and the account may be closed automatically if several installments in a row are missed.
- Premature withdrawal is allowed but attracts a penalty similar to Fixed Deposit.
- Interest earned is taxable as per the customer's income tax slab.
- A loan against the RD balance is usually available in case of financial emergencies, instead of breaking the deposit early.

## How to apply
- Online: Log in to net banking or the mobile app, go to "Deposits," select "Open Recurring Deposit," set the monthly amount and tenure, confirm with OTP.
- Offline: Visit the nearest branch with required documents; branch staff can help set up auto-debit for the monthly installment.
- Nearest branch details can be provided by the assistant if requested.
