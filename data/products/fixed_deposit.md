# Fixed Deposit

## Overview
A Fixed Deposit (FD) is a savings tool where a customer deposits a lump sum amount for a fixed period and earns a fixed interest rate, higher than a regular savings account. The money is locked in for the chosen tenure, and the customer receives the principal plus interest at maturity.

## Eligibility & documents required
- Any individual with a savings account at the bank, or new customers opening one alongside the FD.
- Minimum age: 18 years (minors allowed with a guardian).
- Documents required: PAN card, Aadhaar card, one passport-size photo, and a filled FD application form.
- Minimum deposit amount: ₹1,000. No upper limit.

## Interest rates / returns
- Tenure 7 days to 1 year: 5.5% per annum
- Tenure 1 year to 3 years: 6.5% per annum
- Tenure 3 years to 5 years: 6.8% per annum
- Tenure above 5 years: 7.0% per annum
- Senior citizens receive an additional 0.5% on all tenures.
- Interest can be paid monthly, quarterly, or reinvested at maturity (cumulative option).

## Risks / things to know
- Premature withdrawal is allowed but attracts a penalty of 1% on the applicable interest rate.
- Interest earned is taxable as per the customer's income tax slab.
- FD interest rates are fixed at the time of booking and do not change even if market rates move during the tenure.
- TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) applies if interest earned exceeds ₹40,000 in a financial year (₹50,000 for senior citizens).

## How to apply
- Online: Log in to net banking or the mobile app, go to "Deposits," select "Open Fixed Deposit," enter amount and tenure, confirm with OTP.
- Offline: Visit the nearest branch with required documents and fill out the FD application form; branch staff can assist with tenure and payout selection.
- Nearest branch details can be provided by the assistant if requested.
